Why we travel

Direct trade isn’t a spreadsheet—it’s a handshake.

Every itinerary leg is an excuse to brew with the producers who trust us with their harvests. On the ground we calibrate quality protocols, troubleshoot logistics, and plan resilient futures together.

Farmer-first contracts

We commit to multi-year pricing indexed to farm costs, with shared upside when cup scores climb. The journey map keeps those promises visible for the team and our guests.

Open roasting data

Every stop feeds live metrics—moisture, density, organic certs—straight into our Antwerp roasters. Transparent logs mean buyers know exactly what changed from harvest to release.

Carbon-aware logistics

We bundle lots and coordinate sailings to shrink footprint. The curved paths here mirror the multimodal routes we build to keep coffee fresh without flying every pallet.

Roast lab

Measure everything, repeat what sings.

01

Medellín, Colombia

Harvest calibration

Home base. We align water profiles with partner mills, lock in fermentation targets, and roast sample batches that set the sensory compass for the trip.

02

Jardín Coffee Farm

Everything changed

After two weeks in the coffee shop, we visited a coffee farm. Standing on a hill with freshly squeezed mandarin juice overlooking the coffee hills, I decided: I will work in coffee for the rest of my life.

03

Café Velvet, Medellín

Age 18 · Learning to roast

Returned to Colombia and started working in Café Velvet roastery. I learned to roast, pack coffee, and do green coffee selection—removing defects from parchment to prepare the best coffee for roasting.

04

Coffee Farm Work

One month on the farm

Worked on a coffee farm learning to harvest and process coffee. Learned washed process, drying on industrial scale and African beds, honey process, infused coffees, and much more.

05

Brussels, Belgium

Returning home

After five months in Colombia working as a barista and sometimes roasting at Café Velvet, I came back to Brussels to work in their coffee shop as a barista.

06

Philadelphia, USA

Elixr Coffee

Apprenticeship at Elixr Coffee, a specialty-only roastery. Here I really learned how to roast. We did production roasting with large volumes, and I started cleaning machines for the first time.

07

Schallenberg

Becoming a technician

Started apprenticeship with Schallenberg. I came with two left hands, knowing nothing about screws or nuts. Left as a coffee technician—repairing espresso machines, maintaining roasters, refurbishing equipment.

08

SCA Course

Meeting Andre Godina

When Andre Godina came to teach a coffee course, I was his assistant—preparing all beverages for cupping and espressos. Here I got in contact with SCA and started my first modules.

09

Nairobi, Kenya

Q-grading course

Met Mobula from Utake Coffee. I decided to travel Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda, and Rwanda for three months, assisting Q-grading courses and roasting all the coffee for them.

10

East Africa

Farm to farm

Travelled through four countries by bus, going from coffee farm to coffee farm. A wonderful time discovering new coffees and the different African coffee culture.

11

Honduras

Umami Coffee

Went with Andre to Honduras for a bean-to-cup coffee campus. Andre and other professionals started Umami—a 30-hectare coffee farm. I became a partner and now travel once a year to work there.

12

EBS Europe

Teaching

Became a teacher for EBS Europe, teaching five-day barista courses in five schools. Covered theoretical lessons, hands-on filter brewing, and especially espresso machines and milk steaming.

13

COVID-19 Pivot

Time to change

After seven years of constant travel, COVID stopped everything. I couldn't travel. This forced the decision to pursue my dream: Alaska to Patagonia.

14

Los Angeles, USA

Finding my Subaru

Flew to Los Angeles and found my trusted Subaru. This car would become my home and companion for an incredible three-year journey exploring coffee.

15

Alaska, USA

Journey begins

Drove the Subaru up to Alaska—the starting point. What was planned as one year became three years of freedom, visiting countless coffee farms and building relationships.

16

Mexico

Coffee journey intensifies

From Mexico onward, the coffee journey truly started again. Driving from place to place, visiting farms to improve my knowledge and relationships with farmers.

17

Central America

Building connections

Through Central America, visiting countless coffee farms. Each stop deepened my understanding of coffee production and strengthened relationships.

18

Patagonia

Journey's end

Reached Patagonia after three years on the road. The original one-year plan extended into an incredible adventure of freedom, learning, and connection.

19

Stricktly Coffee

Starting my own venture

After working for others for years, it's time to start my own business. Stricktly Coffee offers barista training, equipment maintenance and installation, and consultancy across the coffee industry.
